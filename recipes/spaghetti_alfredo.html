Spaghetti Alfredo

Description

A homemade italian dish with a twist: we're gonna use frozen ingredients because we are lazy and hunrgy.

My dad used to make this for me all the time as a kid. Hearty, filling, easy to make, and most importantly, CHEAP. This was a staple meal of my childhood.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Frozen Peas
  • Microwavable Bacon
  • Alredo Sauce

Steps

  1. Fill a pot with water and set it on the stovetop to boil.
  2. Once the water has begun to boil, pour in your spaghetti. Stir in the strands until they bend and allow for the entire stalk to be submerged.
  3. Boil spaghetti for 10-12 minutes, or until cooked all the way through.
  4. Once spaghetti has finished cooking, strain out all the water and keep 1 cup of water and set aside
  5. Pour in your alfredo sauce, your spaghetti water, and stir together with your spaghetti. Set stovetop to simmer.
  6. Grab a ceramic bowl and fill it with the desired amount of peas. Place a wet paper towel over the top and microwave for the duration specified by the packaging.
  7. Grab a plate and set down 2 layers of paper towels. Place 3-5 strips of bacon down on top. Cover with another paper towel and microwave for 40-45 seconds.
  8. Pour in your peas and mix together with the spaghetti and alfredo sauce.
  9. Rip bacon into pieces of desired size and scatter into the pot. Mix to get an even distribution.
  10. Grab a pair of tongs and plate.
  11. Serve and enjoy!
